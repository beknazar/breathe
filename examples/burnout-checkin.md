# Example: Burnout Check-In

## User
"I feel exhausted but I haven't even done anything productive today"

## Claude (using Breathe skill)

That disconnect — feeling drained without feeling productive — is one of the earliest burnout signals. Let's do a quick check-in.

**Question 1:** On a scale of 1-10, how energized do you feel about your work right now?

*[User: "Like a 3"]*

**Question 2:** When was the last time you took a real break — no screens, no thinking about work?

*[User: "I don't remember, maybe last weekend?"]*

**Question 3:** Are you sleeping 7+ hours? Be honest.

*[User: "More like 5-6"]*

**Question 4:** Have you been outside today? Sunlight on your face?

*[User: "No"]*

**Question 5:** Who have you talked to — actual voice, not text — in the last 48 hours?

*[User: "Hmm, nobody really"]*

---

Here's what I'm seeing: you're running on empty across the board. The tiredness isn't laziness — it's your body telling you the basics aren't covered.

**Your prescription (in priority order):**

1. **Tonight: sleep.** 7-8 hours minimum. Set an alarm for bedtime, not just morning. Try the 4-7-8 breathing technique before bed (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8 — 4 rounds).

2. **Tomorrow morning: sunlight.** Within 60 minutes of waking, go outside for 10 minutes. Not through a window — actually outside. This resets your cortisol and melatonin cycle.

3. **Today: one real conversation.** Call someone. Not about work. Just a human voice. 10 minutes.

4. **This week: move your body.** Even a 15-minute walk counts. 80 minutes per week of moderate movement reduces burnout markers significantly.

The coding can wait. The Slack messages can wait. You can't pour from an empty cup and right now your cup is bone dry.

Want me to check in with you tomorrow?
