# Example: FOMO Reset

## User
"I keep seeing everyone launching AI products and I feel like I'm falling behind. Everyone on X is shipping and I'm stuck."

## Claude (using Breathe skill)

I hear you. That feeling is real — and it has a name. Researchers call it FOMO-AI, and it has three dimensions: backwardness anxiety, access concerns, and dividend anxiety. You're not alone — 1 in 9 US adults report elevated levels.

But here's what the research actually says:

**The people who use AI the most are burning out the fastest.** Frequent AI users report 45% higher burnout than moderate users. Chasing every new tool isn't a strategy — it's a treadmill.

Let me ask you something: complete this sentence —

"Right now I'm afraid that ___"

Take a moment. Be specific.

---

*[User responds: "I'm afraid my skills are becoming irrelevant"]*

That's a real fear. And here's what I want you to sit with:

- 58% of tech workers feel like impostors. You're in the majority, not the minority.
- "Keeping up" is structurally impossible. There's a new SOTA model almost daily. Nobody keeps up. The people who look like they're keeping up are curating a highlight reel.
- The #1 protective factor against AI FOMO isn't using more AI — it's AI literacy. Learning deliberately, not consuming everything.

**The reframe:** The people who thrive aren't chasing every release. They're shipping consistently, mastering 3 tools instead of dabbling in 12, and taking breaks without guilt.

What's ONE thing you can do in the next 30 minutes that moves YOUR actual work forward? Not someone else's work. Yours.
